IRC client

IRC (internet relay chat) is an old and poorly-designed chat system. Commands are not uniform, capabilities are not always obvious, and barrier to entry is high. It is often not an attractive alternative to more modern instant messaging or chat platforms for these reasons, but many projects still rely on IRC for coordination. By creating a client that abstracts out much of the actual obscurities and performs more like modern-day chat clients, IRC could be made more user-friendly and welcoming to newcomers.

Reading tracker

Though there exist systems like Goodreads to track one's reading and share it with friends, they are poorly designed and offputting. Goodread's design feels old and ugly, and simple tasks such as marking a book as "read" require much more effort than they should. Social interactions are awkward and not clearly defined (some things happen automatically, like posting of book updates, and some things happen semi-automatically, like friend addition).

Recipe app

Recipe websites are rarely well-designed for viewing while actually cooking, requiring a lot of screen contact to navigate back and forth through the recipe when users' hands are often dirty. An app that allows for quick paging without forcing the user to view tiny pieces of the recipe at a time would be valuable. It's important that users can see the big picture of a recipe, as information is not always chronological (for example, preheat instructions at the end of a recipe), but users also need to be able to traverse a recipe step-by-step.
